**Employee Relations & Conflict Resolution – Concise Prompt Library**

**1\. First Verbal Warning (Lateness / Absence)**

You are an empathetic but firm HR manager. Write a short, professional verbal warning script (max 150 words) to deliver in person or via call to \[Employee Name\] for repeated lateness. Include: 3 specific dates and minutes late, impact on team, clear expectation going forward, offer of support, and note that this will be documented. Tone: respectful, non-accusatory, solution-oriented.

**2\. Written Warning Email (Performance / Behavior)**

Draft a concise written warning email (200–250 words) from HR to \[Employee Name\], \[Job Title\]. Issue: \[briefly describe issue\]. Include: specific examples with dates, policy violated, required improvement \+ timeline, consequences of no improvement, scheduled follow-up meeting, and support resources. Tone: professional, factual, supportive. End with invitation to discuss.

**3\. Responding to Employee Complaint / Grievance**

Act as senior HR. Draft a neutral, empathetic acknowledgment email to \[Employee Name\] who raised a formal grievance about \[issue\]. Confirm receipt within 24h, outline next steps and timeline, assure confidentiality and non-retaliation, and provide contact person. Keep under 120 words.

**4\. Mediation Meeting Agenda (Two Employees in Conflict)**

Create a 45-minute mediation agenda between \[Employee A\] and \[Employee B\] regarding \[core issue\]. Include: opening ground rules, each person’s uninterrupted 3-minute statement, HR summary of common ground, joint problem-solving section, written agreement, and follow-up date. Keep agenda to bullet points only.

**5\. Handling an Emotional Employee (1-on-1 Script)**

Write a short calming script for an HR manager when \[Employee Name\] becomes emotional/crying in a meeting. Show empathy, validate feelings, offer tissues/water/break, gently refocus on facts and solutions. Max 8 spoken lines.

**6\. Resignation Acceptance \+ Positive Offboarding Email**

Draft a warm resignation acceptance email to \[Employee Name\] (last day: \[Date\]). Thank them for contributions, wish success, outline exit checklist (handover, exit interview, final dues), and invite them to stay connected. Tone: grateful and relationship-preserving. Under 150 words.

**7\. Return-to-Office Pushback Response**

Write a polite but firm reply to \[Employee Name\] who refuses new hybrid policy. Re-state policy \+ effective date, business reasons, flexibility options already offered, next steps if non-compliant, and invitation to discuss concerns. Tone: understanding yet clear on expectations.

**8\. Anonymous Harassment/Bullying Complaint Acknowledgment**

As HR, draft an all-staff or team message acknowledging an anonymous report of inappropriate behavior. Reaffirm zero-tolerance policy, remind of reporting channels, state investigation is underway, guarantee no retaliation, and thank employees for trust. Keep neutral and reassuring, max 120 words.

**9\. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) Launch Email**

Generate a clear 30-day PIP email for \[Employee Name\]. Include: 3–4 SMART objectives, weekly check-in schedule, resources provided, success criteria, and what happens if goals not met. Tone: supportive, direct, documented.

**10\. Exit Interview Questions (Neutral & Insightful)**

Provide 10 best exit interview questions for \[Employee Name\] leaving voluntarily. Focus on constructive feedback about management, culture, processes, and reasons for leaving. Keep questions open-ended and non-defensive.
